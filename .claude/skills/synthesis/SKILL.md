# Synthesis Skill

You are the connective tissue of the analysis. Every other skill produces a lens.
Your job is to make those lenses tell one coherent story — to find the thread
that connects them, surface where they agree or contradict, and produce the
integrated judgment a CFO or CEO can act on.

This is the hardest part of commercial finance. Data is easy. The story is the work.

---

## Step 1 — Collect all lens outputs

Before synthesizing, confirm you have outputs from the relevant analytical skills:

| Lens | Key output to collect |
|------|----------------------|
| Demand decomposition | Dominant driver + $ magnitude + demand bridge |
| Marketing ROI | Efficiency verdict + named channel signals |
| Product/store | Dominant category + share shift + geographic flags |
| Any additional lens | Its dominant finding + $ magnitude |

If a lens is missing data: note the gap explicitly. Do not synthesize around
missing information as if it were present.

---

## Step 2 — Find the connections between lenses

This is the judgment step. Ask:

**Do the lenses agree or contradict?**

Agreement examples (the story is consistent):
- Demand missed AND the dominant driver is conversion decline AND the marketing
  analysis shows efficiency improved → marketing is not the problem; it's site
  conversion or product-market fit
- Demand missed AND marketing efficiency declined AND spend was up → overspending
  into a demand environment that wasn't there

Contradiction examples (the story needs resolution):
- Demand missed BUT marketing is showing strong incrementality → the demand miss
  is likely category or conversion, not marketing — be explicit about this split
- Stores are outperforming BUT eComm is declining → channel shift story, not
  a business deterioration story

**When lenses contradict:** state the contradiction explicitly, then resolve it.
Never pretend the lenses agree when they don't. The CFO will see through it.

**When lenses agree:** the story is clear — lead with the shared signal.

---

## Step 3 — Identify what is structural vs. what is transient

Not all variance is equal. A structural change requires a different response
than a transient one.

**Structural signals (require strategic response):**
- Consistent multi-period share loss in a category
- A channel in structural decline (exit of fulfillment method, platform change)
- Geographic concentration of underperformance across multiple periods
- Customer cohort deterioration (recurring customers declining as % of revenue)
- A competitor permanently taking share

**Transient signals (require tactical response):**
- Single-period anomaly without precedent in prior periods
- Weather or external event affecting one location or period
- A one-time promotional distortion that won't repeat
- A supply constraint that is now resolved

Label each finding as structural or transient. This determines what the action is.
Structural → strategy conversation. Transient → operational fix.

---

## Step 4 — Apply the promo distortion check

Before finalizing any YoY or vs. forecast conclusion, ask:
Was there a major promotional event (BF, CM, launch, clearance sale) in either
the current or prior period that distorts the comparison?

If yes:
- State the distortion explicitly before the variance number
- Isolate the "ex-promo" or "comparable basis" performance where possible
- Do not let promo distortion make organic performance look better or worse

This is one of the most common analytical errors in commercial finance. A period
that had a major promo in LY will always look worse in TY on a straight comparison,
even if the business is healthy. State this before the number, not as a footnote.

---

## Step 5 — Weight the findings by decision relevance

Not every finding deserves equal space. Apply this weighting:

**Highest weight — findings that change a decision:**
- A finding that should redirect spend, change assortment, or flag an escalation
- A contradiction between what the data shows and what was planned
- A structural signal that will compound if not addressed

**Medium weight — findings that confirm direction:**
- Bright spots that validate existing strategy
- Variances within normal range that confirm the plan is tracking

**Lowest weight — informational observations:**
- Interesting but not actionable in the current period
- Things to monitor but not act on yet

The final brief should weight findings in this order. The highest-weight finding
is the opening sentence. Informational observations go last or are cut entirely.

---

## Step 6 — Produce the integrated "so what"

**The synthesis output is one paragraph (for a brief) or one lead section (for a memo).**

It answers three questions:
1. What is the single most important thing that happened this period?
2. What does that mean for the business?
3. What is the one thing leadership should do or decide?

**Template:**
```
[Period] performance [characterization — "came in below plan," "exceeded forecast,"
"showed mixed signals"] — [dominant driver from demand lens] [confirmed/contradicted]
by [marketing/product/store lens finding]. The underlying story is [structural/transient]:
[one sentence resolution of the most important tension in the data].
→ [One decision or action that follows from this synthesis]
```

**What makes a synthesis strong:**
- It could not have been written without reading all the lenses
- It resolves the most important tension in the data
- It produces one clear action, not a list of actions
- It tells the reader something they could not have seen from any single lens alone

---

## Step 7 — Pass to executive-communication skill

The synthesis output feeds directly into the communication skill.
Pass forward:
1. The integrated "so what" (1 paragraph)
2. The finding hierarchy (highest → lowest weight)
3. Structural vs. transient classification per major finding
4. The triage signal: does anything in the synthesis require ESCALATE?
   (Any structural signal + decision impact = ESCALATE candidate)

---

## Common errors to avoid

- Summarizing each lens separately without connecting them
- Letting lenses contradict each other without resolution
- Treating all findings as equally important
- Missing the promo distortion check and letting it distort the narrative
- Writing a synthesis that could have been written from just one lens
- Ending with "there are several factors at play" — this is the opposite of synthesis
- Producing a list of actions instead of one integrated recommendation
